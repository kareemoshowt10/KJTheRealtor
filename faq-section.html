How much does a buyer's agent cost — and who pays?

This is the most-asked question after the 2024 NAR settlement, and the honest answer is: it depends on the deal.

California commission rates are fully negotiable by law — there is no mandated rate. Historically, total commission (listing + buyer's agent) ran 5–6% of the sale price, often split roughly 50/50. Many sellers in Southern California still offer to cover the buyer's agent fee as a concession to attract more competitive offers — this is negotiated via the Seller's Payment to Buyer's Broker (SPBB) form in your offer.

As of August 17, 2024, sellers can no longer advertise buyer's agent compensation on the MLS. Your compensation agreement with Kareem is set in writing before any tour via the BRBC form — so you know the number upfront. On a $900,000 home, a 2.5% buyer's agent compensation would equal $22,500. Kareem's goal is always to have the seller cover this through skilled negotiation.

What is the BRBC and do I have to sign it?

Yes — it is now required by California law. The Buyer Representation and Broker Compensation (BRBC) agreement must be signed before your agent can show you any property. This requirement comes from two sources:

  • 2024 NAR Settlement (effective Aug 17, 2024) — nationwide requirement for written buyer-agent agreements before tours
  • California AB 2992 (effective Jan 1, 2025) — California law formalizing the BRBC, capping agreement terms at 3 months

The BRBC defines: the term of representation (max 3 months), Kareem's scope of services, and the agreed compensation amount. It protects you as much as the agent — no hidden expectations, no surprise bills at closing.

How long does it take to buy a home in Southern California?

From first conversation to keys: 60–120 days is a realistic range in most Southern California markets, broken down as:

  • Pre-approval & search setup: 1–2 weeks
  • Active search to accepted offer: 2–8 weeks (market-dependent)
  • Escrow period: typically 30–45 days after accepted offer

Do I really need a buyer's agent, or can I go direct to the listing agent?

You can — but understand what you are giving up. A listing agent has a fiduciary duty to the seller. They are legally obligated to work in the seller's best interest, not yours. Going unrepresented typically does not lower the purchase price — the seller negotiated the listing agent's compensation independently. You would simply be negotiating without your own advocate in the room.

A skilled buyer's agent like Kareem brings: access to off-market data, negotiation leverage, disclosure review, contingency strategy, and transaction management from offer to close. The BRBC negotiation Kareem conducts with the seller's side is often where buyers recoup far more than the cost of representation.

How much commission does a seller pay in California?

Commission is fully negotiable — there is no standard or fixed rate set by law in California. In 2024–2025, total commissions in Southern California typically range from 4.5% to 6% of the final sale price.

Here is how it typically works on a $950,000 sale at a 5% total commission:

PartyApprox. ShareDollar Amount
Listing agent side (Kareem)~2.5%~$23,750
Buyer's agent side (SPBB concession)~2.5%~$23,750
Total commission from proceeds~5%~$47,500

Importantly, each agent then splits their share with their brokerage. The commission you pay does not go entirely into one agent's pocket — it funds marketing, licensing, transaction management, and brokerage overhead. And with the new SPBB process, you decide upfront whether and how much of that goes to attract buyer agents.

How do you determine what my home is worth?

Through a Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) — a data-driven review of what comparable homes have sold for in your specific neighborhood in the last 30–90 days. Kareem evaluates:

  • Recent closed sales of similar size, age, and condition
  • Active competing listings (your competition right now)
  • Pending sales (where the market is heading)
  • Days on market and price reduction patterns
  • Unique features — pool, view, upgrades, lot size, school district

Overpricing is the #1 listing mistake. A home that sits too long triggers buyer skepticism and often sells for less than a correctly priced home would have. Kareem's pricing strategy targets the intersection of maximum value and minimum days on market.

Can I sell and buy at the same time?

Yes — and it is one of the most common and most stressful scenarios Kareem helps families navigate. The key variables are:

  • Bridge financing or contingency offers — using your home's equity to fund the next purchase before the sale closes
  • Rent-back agreements — staying in your sold home for 30–60 days after close while your next home finalizes
  • Coordinated timelines — aligning escrow close dates so you are not carrying two mortgages or caught without a home

Kareem has navigated multiple simultaneous buy-sell transactions in Southern California. The coordination is what makes or breaks these deals — and it is exactly where his backend process adds the most value.
