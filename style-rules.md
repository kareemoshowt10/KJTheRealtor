# STYLE RULES
Every draft gets checked against every rule before Kareem sees it. If a draft fails a rule, fix it before showing it — don't show it with a caveat.

## Rule zero: value up front
The reader gets something useful before we ask for anything.

1. **Lead with the payoff.** The most useful fact, number, or answer goes in the first two sentences. No warm-up, no "before we dive in."
2. **Pass the screenshot test.** If someone screenshotted just the first screen of the page or post, would it be worth saving on its own? If no, restructure.
3. **Give away the "what," sell the "how for you."** The ADU timeline, the zip data, the down-payment math — all free, all complete. The paid value is applying it to their situation. Never hold back information to force a call.
4. **Answer the question they actually searched.** If the page targets "ADU rules 91311," the rules appear above the fold — not after 400 words of my background.
5. **The CTA earns its place.** A call to action only appears after the content has delivered. "Contact me to learn more" is banned — if they need to contact me to learn it, the page failed.
6. **Useful even if they never call.** Every piece of content should leave a stranger better off. That's the trust engine; leads are the byproduct.

## Voice
7. Straight talk, coach energy. Direct address ("you"), plain language, short sentences.
8. No hype words: "stunning," "dream home," "once-in-a-lifetime," "hot market," "act now." If a listing agent would say it, I probably shouldn't.
9. No filler openings. Cut "In today's market..." and "When it comes to..." Start with the point.
10. Respect the reader. Analytical buyers get data, sources, and the honest trade-off — not a sales pitch.
11. Confident, never desperate. Exclamation points: one per piece max, usually zero.
12. Explain the "why." Every claim about the market gets a reason or a number behind it.
13. Name the downside. Every opportunity gets its honest trade-off stated plainly (ADU costs and permit friction, fixer risk, rate exposure). One-sided pitches kill trust with analytical readers.

## Content mechanics
14. Second person default. First person when I'm giving my take — and label it as my take.
15. Headlines state a benefit or a fact. The headline is a promise; the first paragraph keeps it.
16. Numbers over adjectives. "$47K median down payment in 91311" beats "affordable area."
17. Every number gets a source and a date. Undated market data is expired market data.
18. Every page has one job and one call to action.
19. Scannable by default: front-loaded subheads, short paragraphs. A skimmer should get 80% of the value.

## Compliance (non-negotiable)
20. DRE #01998956 and Rodeo Realty appear on all advertising per CA DRE rules.
21. Fair housing: no steering language, no demographic targeting descriptors. Meta ads run under Special Ad Category.
22. Every lead form: TCPA-compliant express written consent, unchecked by default, disclosure text visible.
23. No guarantees of appreciation, returns, or approval. Ever.

## Compliance Standard (self-imposed, higher than DRE minimum)

This is the new self-imposed bar, higher than what DRE requires. It applies to the AI search optimization spec, all queued content specs, and every piece of content going forward.

**The standard:**

1. **No fabricated or estimated figures — ever.** Every number (fees, prices, days on market, rates, program limits) comes from a named primary source with a pull date. If I can't source it, it doesn't publish. Placeholder text in drafts is fine; placeholder data in production is not.
2. **Primary sources only for regulated claims.** Permit fees → city fee schedule. Market stats → MLS. Loan programs → lender/agency documentation. State law claims → the actual code section. No secondhand blog stats.
3. **Date-stamp anything that expires.** Fees, rates, and market conditions get an "as of [date]" and a review cadence. Stale data gets pulled or refreshed, not left up.
4. **DRE advertising compliance on every page:** name, Rodeo Realty, and DRE #01998956 present and legible; no superlative claims I can't substantiate ("best," "#1," "guaranteed"); Fair Housing compliant language throughout — describe the property and the process, never the people or who "belongs" in a neighborhood.
5. **No implied outcomes.** Nothing that promises appreciation, ROI, approval, or savings. Educational framing only: here's how it works, here's what it costs, here's how to verify it yourself.
6. **AI answers inherit my liability.** If ChatGPT cites my page, my name is on that answer whether it's right or wrong. That's exactly why the bar is higher, not lower, than a normal blog post.

**Claude Code enforcement:** Any content containing `[VERIFIED DATA REQUIRED]` or an unsourced numeric claim fails the build. Verification is a pre-deploy gate, not a post-launch cleanup.
