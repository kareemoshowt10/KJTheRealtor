The 93063 Market

Why buyers go east first.

The 93063 zip code is East Simi Valley — everything roughly east of Sycamore Drive, running to the Santa Susana Pass and the LA County line. It's where Simi Valley began: the historic Santa Susana township, the old movie-ranch country around Corriganville Park, and the oak-studded hillsides of the Knolls. Today it's the city's best-kept commuter secret — the first exit off the pass, which means 10–15 minutes saved each way versus the west end for anyone working in the Valley or LA.

The inventory has real range. Big Sky delivers newer construction and panoramic views beneath Rocky Peak. Santa Susana Knolls is one of Ventura County's most distinctive pockets — rustic custom homes on large, wooded lots with no two houses alike. Texas Canyon and The Knolls offer hillside acreage and privacy. And the Tapo Street and Tapo Canyon corridors carry the value core: established 1960s–70s ranch homes on generous lots, many with genuine remodel and ADU upside.

For long-term buyers the fundamentals hold: Simi Valley's top-10 California safety ranking, Santa Susana High School anchoring the east end, Metrolink's Simi Valley station minutes away, and trail access at Rocky Peak, Corriganville, and Tapo Canyon that LA County equivalents simply can't match at these prices.

Inherited & Probate Homes · Simi Valley

Selling an inherited house in Simi Valley.

Step-up in basis, estate clean-out, and Ventura County probate — a calm path through a complicated process.

East Simi Valley is the oldest part of the city — which means 93063 sees more inherited homes than anywhere else in Simi Valley. Many Tapo-corridor and Santa Susana homes have been family-owned for 30, 40, even 50 years, which makes understanding the tax and probate implications essential before any decisions are made.

The most powerful financial tool for heirs is the step-up in basis. California law, combined with federal tax code, allows the tax basis on an inherited home to reset to its fair market value at the date of death. A Simi Valley home bought for $180,000 in 1990 and worth $900,000 today could be sold with no capital gains tax owed by the heirs — a benefit worth hundreds of thousands of dollars that many families unknowingly leave on the table.

Critically, Simi Valley is in Ventura County — so probate proceedings are handled through Ventura County Superior Court in Oxnard, not the LA County courthouse.
