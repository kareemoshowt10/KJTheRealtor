The Method

The Generational Wealth Method™

Before I recommend a home, price, or offer, we answer four decisions most transactions skip. You leave with a plan you can see, use, and revisit.

No. 01

Position

We define what this move must improve: monthly comfort, commute, schools, space, care needs, or long-term equity.

The decision: Does this home improve your life without weakening your next move?
You receive: a one-page Move Map
No. 02

Protect

We set the walk-away number, contingency strategy, repair priorities, and negotiation limits before emotion takes over.

The decision: What must be true for this deal to remain a smart deal?
You receive: written Offer Guardrails
No. 03

Compound

We model how your equity, payment, ownership timeline, and future options work together after closing—not only on closing day.

The decision: Will this property create flexibility or consume it?
You receive: a 3-horizon Equity Plan
No. 04

Transfer

We identify the ownership, records, family conversations, and licensed advisors needed to make the asset easier to manage and eventually pass on.

The decision: Could your family understand and act on your plan without guessing?
You receive: a Family Handoff Checklist
Use this before you tour

Start your four-line Move Map

Write one sentence for each prompt. These answers become your filter when a beautiful property makes the decision feel emotional.

01 · Life This move must make ______ easier.

Example: caring for my parents, working from home, or giving the kids safe outdoor space.

02 · Money Our comfortable all-in monthly ceiling is ______.

Include principal, interest, taxes, insurance, HOA costs, utilities, and a repair reserve.

03 · Protection We will walk away if ______.

Name the condition now: price, inspection risk, appraisal gap, commute, or lost contingency protection.

04 · Future In five years, this home should help us ______.

Examples: build equity, create rental options, rightsize, relocate, or support family nearby.

The rule: if a property does not support at least three of your four answers, it may be attractive—but it is probably not your move.

A 60-second self-audit

Is your next move actually ready?

If you cannot answer “yes” to all four, the next step is clarity—not another listing alert.

  • I know the maximum monthly cost that still lets my family live well.
  • I know my walk-away price and which protections I will not surrender.
  • I can explain how this move supports my five- and ten-year goals.
  • The right people could locate and understand my property plan.
